# Important Changes and Corrections Made After the Guidebook Was Printed

## Companion notice for 2026 Utah Fishing Guidebook

### Emergency changes

**Crouse Reservoir (Uintah County):**
Because of low water levels and poor snowpack, the daily limit at Crouse Reservoir was increased to 8 trout, any species. This change went into effect on May 11, 2026 and will remain in effect until Dec. 31, 2026.

The Utah Division of Wildlife Resources wants to provide anglers the opportunity to harvest more fish in hopes that reducing the population may:

- Improve survival of the remaining fish.
- Encourage fishing in this underutilized fishery.

All other rules established in the 2026 Utah Fishing Guidebook remain in effect. (May 11, 2026)

**Nine Mile Reservoir (Sanpete County):**
Because of drought-related water losses, the daily limit at Nine Mile Reservoir was increased to 16 trout. This change went into effect on May 11, 2026 and will remain in effect until Oct. 1, 2026.

The Utah Division of Wildlife Resources is concerned that fish will not survive the summer, so it proactively increased the limit to:

- Reduce trout densities and improve survival of any remaining fish.
- Encourage anglers to make use of fish that would otherwise perish.

All other rules established in the 2026 Utah Fishing Guidebook remain in effect. (May 11, 2026)
